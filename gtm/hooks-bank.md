# Hooks bank — SoundCave

Opening lines and content angles for the first-100-users push. A *hook* is the
first line or framing that earns the scroll-stop. Pulled from the ICP's real
language (the agent's listening layer is the source of these patterns).

**How to use:** pick a hook, pair it with a real visual (the product makes its own
proof), and end with a soft path to the waitlist — never lead with the link. Match
the voice in [`src/prompts/draft.md`](../src/prompts/draft.md): plain, specific,
warm, no hype, no emoji-stuffing. Respect each room's
[`promo_policy`](../src/config.py).

Legend — **policy fit:** 🟢 ok in `open` rooms (mention allowed, once, casually) ·
🟡 `help_only`/`strict` (help only, no product mention) · ⭐ best for X build-in-public.

## By hook type

### 1. Pain-point (name the ache they already feel)
- 🟡 "Spent more time fighting Canva than making the actual track this week. Anyone else?"
- 🟢 "A designer quoted me £150 for a single cover for a song 40 people will hear. There has to be a better way — here's what I do now."
- 🟡 "The worst part of releasing isn't the music. It's needing a cover, a square, a story, a banner — all by Friday."
- ⭐ "Every indie artist hits the same wall: the music's done, and now you need 6 pieces of artwork you can't afford. Thread on how I stopped dreading it 🧵"

### 2. Show-don't-tell (the visual is the argument)
- 🟢 "Made 10 EP covers in the time it took my coffee to go cold. Which one's the single? [grid]"
- ⭐ "Same song, 5 different cover styles in 2 minutes. Before/after of my actual release →"
- ⭐ "'AI cover art' usually looks like AI. These don't. [carousel] — here's the style I used."
- 🟡 "Someone asked how I did my gig poster — here's the before (phone photo) and after, plus the steps."

### 3. Build-in-public (the founder story — strongest on X) ⭐
- ⭐ "I'm building SoundCave: type your track, get release-ready covers and flyers in seconds. Day 1 of finding our first 100 users. Follow along."
- ⭐ "I built an AI agent that listens in music subreddits and tells me exactly what artwork artists are struggling with. Here's what it found this week 🧵"
- ⭐ "Goal: 100 real users in 4 weeks, zero ad spend, one founder. Here's the playbook I'm running in public."
- ⭐ "Watch me make a full release pack — cover, single art, IG square, story — for one song, live, in under 3 minutes."

### 4. Contrarian / strong-opinion (earns replies)
- ⭐ "Hot take: most indie artists lose more fans to bad artwork than to bad mixing. Your cover is the first 0.5 seconds."
- 🟢 "You don't need a designer for your first 10 releases. You need a fast way to not look amateur. Different problem."
- ⭐ "Stop paying £200 for cover art before you've proven the song. Spend it on the song. Make the cover in minutes."

### 5. Curiosity / question (low-effort, high-reply, great for AEO) 🟡
- 🟡 "Producers/DJs: where do you actually get your cover art and flyers? Trying to map what people use."
- 🟡 "What's the one piece of release artwork you always end up rushing at the last minute?"
- 🟡 "Honest question — how much do you spend per release on visuals, and is it worth it?"

> Curiosity hooks double as **answer-engine bait**: they're the exact phrasings
> LLMs surface when someone asks "best way to make cover art". A genuinely useful,
> upvoted reply is an earned citation.

## By segment

| Segment | Angle that lands | Example hook |
|---|---|---|
| DJ / electronic producer | Recurring gig/club flyers, fast turnaround | 🟢 "I run a weekly — I was redoing the flyer every Monday. Here's how I cut it to 5 minutes." |
| Event / club-night promoter | Volume + consistency across a series | ⭐ "12 events, one visual identity, no designer on retainer. The system I use." |
| Small label / artist manager | Many releases, tight budget, brand consistency | 🟢 "Managing 4 artists means 4× the artwork. Here's how I keep it consistent without 4× the cost." |
| Indie artist (non-dance) | First release, looks-amateur fear | ⭐ "Your first single deserves a cover that doesn't scream 'first single'. Here's the cheap way to get there." |

## AEO/GEO target questions (win these via earned mentions)

Head questions the ICP asks Google *and* LLMs — answer them genuinely and
SoundCave becomes part of the answer:

- "how to make cover art for a single"
- "cheap cover art for music / affordable album artwork"
- "AI cover art that doesn't look AI"
- "how do DJs make their event flyers"
- "best tool to make music release graphics"
- "Canva alternative for musicians"

For each: post one genuinely useful Reddit answer + one short YouTube/X demo. Track
which ones get cited (see the measurement note in
[growth-strategy.md](../wiki/spec/growth-strategy.md)).

## Call-to-action endings (soft, waitlist-bound)

Never lead with these; close with them, once.
- "I'm putting this in a tool — early access list is in my bio if useful."
- "Building this into SoundCave; waitlist's open if you want in." *(🟢 open rooms only)*
- "Happy to share the exact steps — DM me." *(🟡 help-only rooms: no link, just help)*
